What is SunDAI and how does it work?
▼

pSunDAI is a $1-pegged stablecoin backed by PLS — built entirely on-chain with no owner, no admin keys, and no upgrades ever possible. It gives PulseChain users a stable asset without relying on a bank or company.

You lock PLS as collateral at at least 150% of the value you mint. This is called a Collateralized Debt Position (CDP). If you deposit $150 of PLS, you can mint up to $100 of pSunDAI. Your collateral stays locked until you repay the pSunDAI.

A tiny 0.5% annual stability fee accrues on your debt — $1000 borrowed becomes $1005 after one year. If PLS price drops and your ratio falls below 110%, liquidators can partially repay your debt and claim your PLS at a discount. Keep your ratio healthy to avoid this.

Repay & Exit
Repay pSunDAI
Reduces Debt
When should I repay?

Burns pSunDAI to reduce your debt and raise your ratio. Instant — no cooldown. Use this whenever PLS is falling and your ratio approaches 150%, or when you want to exit the vault.

Two helpful shortcuts: Repay to Safe auto-calculates the exact amount to bring you back to exactly 150%. Repay & Withdraw repays your specified amount then automatically returns any PLS above 150% to your wallet.

Repaying requires two transactions: an approval (authorise the vault to spend your pSunDAI) followed by the repayment itself.

What limits my withdrawal?

Withdraws PLS from your vault. The protocol only allows withdrawal of collateral that keeps your ratio above 150%. So the maximum you can take out is whatever is "excess" above that minimum.

There is a 5-minute cooldown after depositing before you can withdraw — to protect against flash-loan attacks.

Use Withdraw Max Safe to automatically take all the PLS you can while staying exactly at 150%. Useful for taking profits when PLS pumps.

Safety & Advanced
Liquidation Dashboard
What can I do there?

The dashboard shows all vaults across the system that have fallen below 110% and are eligible for liquidation. If you hold pSunDAI, you can repay another user's debt and receive their PLS at a 2–5% discount (the bonus grows over time, Dutch-auction style).

Your own vault's liquidation risk is shown in the ratio gauge above — keep it green and you have nothing to worry about.

When can I use this?

If you have zero debt and your last deposit was over 30 days ago, you can force-withdraw all PLS. This is a failsafe to prevent accidental permanent lockups in the immutable contract.
