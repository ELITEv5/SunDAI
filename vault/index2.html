What is SunDAI and how does it work?
▼

pSunDAI is a $1-pegged stablecoin backed by PLS — built entirely on-chain with no owner, no admin keys, and no upgrades ever possible. It gives PulseChain users a stable asset without relying on a bank or company.

You lock PLS as collateral at at least 150% of the value you mint. This is called a Collateralized Debt Position (CDP). If you deposit $150 of PLS, you can mint up to $100 of pSunDAI. Your collateral stays locked until you repay the pSunDAI.

A 0.5% annual stability fee accrues on your debt — $1,000 borrowed becomes $1,005 after one year. If PLS price drops and your ratio falls below 110%, liquidators can partially repay your debt and claim your PLS at a 2–5% discount.

Flash crash protection: The oracle ignores price drops shorter than 4 hours. A real market drop takes 4+ hours to confirm, then moves the price in 5% steps every 30 minutes. Your vault will not be flash-liquidated.

What is the oracle and why does it matter?

The oracle is the on-chain price feed that tells the vault the current PLS/USD rate. It takes the median price across five PulseX trading pairs (DAI v1/v2, USDC v1/v2, USDT) using a TWAP + spot hybrid calculation.

Price changes under 3% are accepted instantly. Larger moves require a confirmation period before taking effect: 4 hours for price drops and 1 hour for pumps. After confirmation, prices step toward the target at 5% per 30 minutes downward or 10% per 30 minutes upward. This means a flash crash lasting less than 4 hours cannot liquidate your vault.

Manual Wake Oracle triggers a price update. There is a 30-minute cooldown between pokes. The vault always falls back to the last valid price if the oracle is silent, so you can always repay and withdraw.
